Thermoelectric Materials

Wikipedia - Thermoelectric Materials Nature - Thermoelectric Materials

Thermoelectric materials are materials that can convert heat into electricity and vice versa. They have important applications in power generation, cooling, and temperature sensing.

Applications

  • Power generation in space probes and remote locations.
  • Waste heat recovery in industrial processes.
  • Portable thermoelectric generators for charging devices.

Advantages

  • Highly reliable and durable.
  • No moving parts, making them low maintenance.
  • Environmentally friendly energy conversion.

Challenges

  • Efficiency improvements are ongoing.
  • Cost can be a limiting factor for some applications.
  • Optimizing materials for specific temperature ranges is complex.
Property Description
Seebeck Effect Thermoelectric materials exhibit the Seebeck effect, generating an electric voltage when there's a temperature difference between their two sides.
